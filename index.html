Ruth Bader Ginsburg

"The Notorious R.B.G."

  • Ruth Bader Ginsburg was born in Brooklyn, NYC in 1933.
  • R.B.G. taught at Rutgers University Law School, where she was first inspired to join the women's rights movement.
  • R.B.G taught at Columbia University, and there, she bacame its first female tenured professor.
  • In the 1970s, R.B.G. served as the director of the Women's Rights Project of the American Civil Liberties Union.
  • In 1980, Ginsburg was appointed to the U.S. Court of Appeals for the District of Columbia.
  • In 1993, Ginsburg was nominated to the U.S. Supreme Court by President Bill Clinton. As the second woman to become a Supreme Court Justice, Ginsburg continued to argue on behalf of gender equality.
  • Ruth Bader Ginsburg died on September 18, 2020 due to complications from metastatic pancreas cancer.

"I pray that I may be all that my mother would have been had she lived in an age when women could aspire and achieve and daughters are cherished as much as sons."

- Ruth Bader Ginsburg
